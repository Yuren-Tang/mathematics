## 2. Physical response and gauge duality

For the route-capped carrier

$$
F=\mathcal L(\widehat Q),
$$

full cocycles fit into

$$
0\to\operatorname{Cut}(\widehat Q)
\to\operatorname{Cocycle}(M_\tau(F))
\to\operatorname{Cycle}(\widehat Q)\to0.
$$

Writing a cocycle as `(z,a,z+a)`, the quotient functional is the mod-two ribbon intersection form

$$
\mathcal B(z,z')=a\cdot z'.
$$

Its radical is boundary homology and its rank is Euler genus. A Petrial word `\lambda` adds

$$
(z,z')\longmapsto
\sum_e\lambda_ez_ez'_e.
$$

For an admissible gauge code `C`, a cycle `z` is radicalisable exactly when

$$
[a]\in\operatorname{Im}
\bigl(\lambda\mapsto[\lambda\odot z]\bigr).
$$

Failure is certified by

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal B(z,z')=1,
\qquad
w=z\odot z'\in C^\perp.
}
$$

For the Fano-flow gauge code,

$$
C^\perp
=
\mathcal C(G)*\mathcal F_f.
$$

## 3. Hyperbolic norm hierarchy

Every persistent word has a dual-flow representation

$$
\boxed{
w_e=\langle u(e),f(e)\rangle,}
$$

where `u:E\to\Gamma^*` is a flow. Thus `(f,u)` is a flow in the hyperbolic space `\Gamma\oplus\Gamma^*`, and `w` is its quadratic norm support.

Because an odd-intersection pair of cubic cycles cannot be edge-disjoint,

$$
w\ne0.
$$

The minimum span dimension of `u(E)` is the dual rank, equal to `1`, `2`, or `3`.

## 4. Dual rank one: localised

In rank one,

$$
w=c*(\alpha\circ f)
$$

for one binary cycle `c` and one scalar sheet `H_\alpha`.

On every scalar circuit, the support is empty, the whole circuit, or an alternating union of marked intervals. The odd ribbon-intersection bit decomposes over connected components, so one component alone is odd.

Hence rank one reduces to:

$$
\boxed{
\text{one odd scalar circuit}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\text{one odd marked scalar interval}.
}
$$

The remaining rank-one problem is composition/root-admissibility, not localisation.

## 5. Dual rank two: localised

Let the image plane of `u` have annihilator colour `h`. Then the norm support is controlled by the two four-flows

$$
\bar f:E\to\Gamma/\langle h\rangle,
\qquad
u:E\to(\Gamma/\langle h\rangle)^*.
$$

At a vertex where both triples are nowhere zero, they define a permutation of the three nonzero quotient colours. The norm support is its moved-point set. Since `w=z\odot z'` cannot use all three edges of a cubic star, the permutation is identity or a transposition, never a three-cycle.

Therefore every odd-degree endpoint of `w` lies at:

- the Fano colour matching `E_h`; or
- the zero-value locus of the auxiliary four-flow `u`.

One connected component carries the odd intersection. Thus rank two reduces to:

$$
\boxed{
\text{one odd closed norm circuit}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\text{one odd defect-to-defect path}.
}
$$

Again the remaining issue is physical composition through the endpoint semantics.

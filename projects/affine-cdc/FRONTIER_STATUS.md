## 4. Nonflat branch

Open: compare this scalar-sheet object with the original source four-pole and extract bounded, connected, composition-safe progress.

## 5. Flat branch

## 6. Type T and Type H

Type T requires serialisation or another strict source decomposition.

Type H has a complete individual-loop soluble escape theorem, but the family-level obstructed branch still requires one coherent physical witness. Individual ambient conjugacies do not provide a simultaneous BBD origin.

## 8. Target frontier

Target capacity is exact for dominating cliques of ranks two through five and for eight-vertex factorable quotients. Rank-one and non-dominating full-dual obstruction families remain incomplete.

Target completeness alone would not prove source composition.

## 9. Certificate boundary

Finite tables do not close any of the six gaps. In particular:

- BBD monodromy counts do not prove groupoid closure;
- defect/Petersen tables do not define a valid variation slice;
- `7737` composite endpoints do not prove connected escape;
- small four-pole census does not prove the full-cap theorem;
- scalar-sheet cuts do not prove source localization.

## 10. Global endpoint

The global five-support theorem remains open. The missing implication is:

$$
\text{persistent vertically and horizontally bad data}
\Longrightarrow
\text{good escape or strict source progress}.
$$

Strict progress means a smaller separator, bounded replacement, composition-safe factor, or exact gluing theorem. Programme B9/global assembly remains blocked on this implication.

# AffineCDC: mathematical architecture

**Architectural status:** controlling synthesis.  This document states how the active mathematical results fit together.  Detailed proofs and computations remain in the linked theorem dossiers.

## 0. The thesis

The project begins with a local-to-global compatibility problem attached to a nowhere-zero binary flow on a cubic graph.

At every cubic vertex, the three incident flow values form a binary plane.  They determine several local affine families of even dart selections.  The global task is to choose one family at each vertex so that the two local labels on every edge agree.  Such a compatible choice reconstructs an indexed family of cycles in which every dart occurs once, hence a cycle double cover.

The mathematical story has four levels:

1. a rank-independent affine incidence problem;
2. a rank-three quadratic-Lagrangian theorem forcing compatibility;
3. a higher-rank residue theory explaining exactly how compatibility can fail;
4. reductions and consequences: quotient sheaves, stresses, tensor/code homology, rigidity, cut quotients, gluing, and topological state spaces.

The first two levels are the AffineCDC spine.  The others are genuine mathematics generated by the spine, but they are not alternative centres.

## 1. Source geometry and the output problem

Let \(G=(V,E)\) be a finite connected cubic graph and let

\[
f:E\to\Gamma
\]

be a nowhere-zero flow over a finite-dimensional binary vector space \(\Gamma\).  At a vertex \(v\), the three incident values are distinct nonzero vectors

\[
h_1+h_2+h_3=0
\]

and hence are the three nonzero points of a plane

\[
W_v\leq\Gamma.
\]

For every edge \(e\), define the coefficient quotient

\[
Q_e=\Gamma/\langle f(e)\rangle.
\]

The local affine classification associates to \(v\) a family of quotient labels on the three incidences \((v,e)\).  If \(\kappa_{v,e}\in Q_e\) is the common class of the other two incident flow values, every local affine family has the form

\[
(\kappa_{v,e}+[m_v]_e)_{e\ni v},
\qquad m_v\in\Gamma.
\]

The local parameter \(m_v\) is not itself the invariant datum; the quotient-label triple is.

A global choice is compatible when the two endpoint labels on every edge coincide.  The source graph and its dart structure are still required after compatibility has been solved: the quotient labels must be interpreted as local even dart families and then followed through the graph to obtain global cycles.  Consequently no reduced linear object should be described as remembering the whole CDC construction.

The honest source-to-output chain is

\[
(G,\Gamma,f)
\longrightarrow
\text{local affine families}
\longrightarrow
\text{compatible global family}
\longrightarrow
\text{cycle double cover}.
\]

The middle arrow is the algebraic problem studied below.

## 2. The universal compatibility core

### 2.1 Incidence space

Let

\[
I(G)=\{(v,e):v\in e\}
\]

and define

\[
E_f=\bigoplus_{(v,e)\in I(G)}Q_e.
\]

This space has two natural decompositions: by vertices and by the two endpoint copies of each edge.

At a vertex define

\[
\Delta_v:\Gamma\to\bigoplus_{e\ni v}Q_e,
\qquad
m\mapsto([m]_e)_{e\ni v}.
\]

The map is injective because the three incident flow lines have zero common intersection.  Put

\[
L_v=\operatorname{im}\Delta_v,
\qquad
L_{\mathrm{vert}}=\bigoplus_v L_v.
\]

Let \(\kappa\in E_f\) be the tuple of all \(\kappa_{v,e}\).  Then

\[
C_{\mathrm{vert}}=\kappa+L_{\mathrm{vert}}
\]

is precisely the space of all independent choices of one local affine family at every vertex.

For an edge \(e=uv\), define its diagonal

\[
D_e=\{(x,x):x\in Q_e\}
\le Q_e^{(u)}\oplus Q_e^{(v)}
\]

and put

\[
L_{\mathrm{edge}}=\bigoplus_eD_e.
\]

Membership in \(L_{\mathrm{edge}}\) is exactly endpoint matching.

Thus the smallest honest compatibility datum is

\[
\mathfrak I_f=
(E_f,L_{\mathrm{vert}},L_{\mathrm{edge}},
\kappa+L_{\mathrm{vert}}).
\]

### 2.2 The affine incidence-pair complex

The tuple becomes conceptually complete when written as the two-term complex

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal P_f:
L_{\mathrm{vert}}\oplus L_{\mathrm{edge}}
\xrightarrow{d}
E_f,
\qquad d(l,m)=l+m.
}
\]

Its cohomology is

\[
H^0(\mathcal P_f)
\cong L_{\mathrm{vert}}\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}},
\]

\[
H^1(\mathcal P_f)
\cong E_f/(L_{\mathrm{vert}}+L_{\mathrm{edge}}).
\]

The inhomogeneous equation

\[
d(l,m)=\kappa
\]

is equivalent to

\[
(\kappa+L_{\mathrm{vert}})\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}}\neq\varnothing.
\]

Therefore:

- the compatibility obstruction is \([\kappa]\in H^1(\mathcal P_f)\);
- compatibility is \([\kappa]=0\);
- when compatible, the solution set is a torsor under \(H^0(\mathcal P_f)\).

This is rank-independent linear algebra.  It separates the existence problem from any special rank-three mechanism used to solve it.

### 2.3 Quotient-sheaf presentation

Cancelling the contractible edge-diagonal summand gives the quotient presentation

\[
L_{\mathrm{vert}}
\longrightarrow
E_f/L_{\mathrm{edge}}.
\]

Using

\[
L_{\mathrm{vert}}\cong\Gamma^V,
\qquad
E_f/L_{\mathrm{edge}}
\cong\bigoplus_eQ_e,
\]

this becomes

\[
\delta_f:\Gamma^V\to\bigoplus_eQ_e,
\qquad
(\delta_fm)_e=[m_u+m_v].
\]

The affine equation is

\[
\delta_fm=c_f.
\]

Thus the flow quotient sheaf is not a separate theory of compatibility; it is the asymmetric coordinate presentation of the symmetric pair complex.

### 2.4 Dual presentation: equilibrium stresses

Dualizing the incidence pair gives

\[
L_{\mathrm{vert}}^\perp\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}}^\perp.
\]

The vertex orthogonality condition says that the three incident covectors sum to zero; the edge orthogonality condition says that the two endpoint covectors agree.  Their intersection is exactly the equilibrium-stress space

\[
\operatorname{Stress}(f).
\]

The finite-dimensional solvability criterion becomes

\[
[\kappa]=0
\iff
\psi(\kappa)=0
\quad\text{for every equilibrium stress }\psi.
\]

Quotient sheaf and stress theory are therefore primal and dual presentations of one affine incidence complex.

## 3. The rank-three Fano enhancement

Assume now

\[
\Gamma=\mathbf F_2^3.
\]

Then every edge quotient \(Q_e\) is a two-dimensional binary space.  It has a canonical quadratic function

\[
q_e(x)=\mathbf1_{x\neq0}.
\]

Its polarization is the unique nonzero alternating form on \(Q_e\).  If \(\omega\in\Lambda^3\Gamma^*\) is the nonzero volume form, then

\[
B_e([x],[y])=\omega(f(e),x,y).
\]

### 3.1 Local Fano Lagrangian

At a vertex with flow plane \(W\), let

\[
E_W=\bigoplus_{h\in W\setminus\{0\}}
\Gamma/\langle h\rangle,
\qquad
q_W=\sum_hq_h.
\]

The diagonal image

\[
L_W=\{([x]_h)_h:x\in\Gamma\}
\]

is three-dimensional inside the six-dimensional symplectic space \(E_W\).  The Fano identity

\[
\sum_{h\in W\setminus\{0\}}h=0
\]

makes \(L_W\) isotropic, hence Lagrangian.

The restriction of \(q_W\) to this Lagrangian is the unique nonzero functional

\[
\ell_W:\Gamma\to\mathbf F_2,
\qquad
\ker\ell_W=W:
\]

\[
q_W(([x]_h)_h)=\ell_W(x).
\]

This is the local Fano quadratic transgression: a sum of three quotient quadratics collapses to the outside-plane linear bit.

### 3.2 Characteristic torsor

For a Lagrangian \(L\) in a quadratic space \((E,q)\), define

\[
\operatorname{Char}_q(L)=
\{\kappa\in E:B(z,\kappa)=q(z)
\text{ for all }z\in L\}.
\]

It is an affine torsor under \(L\).  In the local Fano space,

\[
\operatorname{Char}_{q_W}(L_W)
=
\kappa_W+L_W,
\]

and this is exactly the space of local affine families.

Thus the primitive local package is

\[
\boxed{(E_W,q_W,L_W,\operatorname{Char}_{q_W}(L_W)).}
\]

It simultaneously contains:

- the local homogeneous family space;
- the affine classification;
- legal dual configurations;
- the cross-bit;
- support parity.

The earlier case-by-case branching statements are projections of this package.

### 3.3 Global Lagrangians

Taking the direct sum over vertices gives a Lagrangian

\[
L_{\mathrm{vert}}\le(E_f,q_f)
\]

whose characteristic torsor is the full space of local affine choices.

For each edge, the endpoint diagonal \(D_e\) is not only Lagrangian but totally singular:

\[
q_e(x)+q_e(x)=0.
\]

Hence

\[
L_{\mathrm{edge}}=\bigoplus_eD_e
\]

is a totally singular Lagrangian.

### 3.4 Affine Lagrangian-intersection theorem

Let \(L,M\) be Lagrangians in a nondegenerate quadratic space.  Then

\[
\operatorname{Char}_q(L)\cap M\neq\varnothing
\iff
q|_{L\cap M}=0.
\]

If \(M\) is totally singular, the condition is automatic.  Applying this with

\[
L=L_{\mathrm{vert}},
\qquad
M=L_{\mathrm{edge}}
\]

gives

\[
\boxed{
\operatorname{Char}_{q_f}(L_{\mathrm{vert}})
\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}}
\neq\varnothing.
}
\]

Therefore the rank-three affine obstruction vanishes.

The solution set is a torsor under

\[
L_{\mathrm{vert}}\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}}
\cong H^0(G;\mathscr Q_f).
\]

### 3.5 Equivalent proof views

Several earlier arguments are not independent proofs but different resolutions of this intersection theorem.

#### Quadratic handshaking

For \(z\in L_{\mathrm{vert}}\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}}\), the same quotient vector occurs at both ends of every edge, so

\[
q_f(z)=2\sum_eq_e(z_e)=0.
\]

This verifies the exact intersection criterion.

#### Support-boundary transgression

For a global section \(k=(k_v)\), define

\[
s(k)_e=q_{f(e)}([k_v]_e).
\]

The local Fano identity gives

\[
(\partial s(k))_v=\ell_{W_v}(k_v).
\]

The augmentation kills every graph boundary, forcing the global obstruction pairing to vanish.

#### Stress cancellation

Under the polar identifications \(Q_e\cong Q_e^*\), the same Lagrangian intersection is also the stress space.  Pairing the canonical affine datum with a stress gives a sum of local quadratic values, and each edge contribution appears twice.

#### Fano Hodge duality

For a Lagrangian pair,

\[
L_{\mathrm{vert}}\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}}
\cong
\bigl(E_f/(L_{\mathrm{vert}}+L_{\mathrm{edge}})\bigr)^*.
\]

This is the source of the global-section/obstruction self-duality.  It is not an accidental dimension equality.

### 3.6 CDC extraction

The intersection theorem supplies a compatible indexed local family.  The source graph and dart involution then assemble the matched local pairs into global cycles.  Each dart appears exactly once in the indexed family, so each undirected edge appears twice.  This is the cycle-double-cover output.

The extraction belongs in the core paper because it connects the algebraic compatibility theorem to the graph-theoretic result.  It should not be hidden inside the reduced tensor language, which forgets the necessary dart interpretation.

## 4. Why rank three is exceptional

The rank-three theorem is not merely a small finite coincidence.  Three independent-looking balances become the same equation.

Let \(\dim\Gamma=r\).

### 4.1 Support degree

The nonzero indicator on a \(d\)-dimensional binary space has algebraic degree \(d\).  Since

\[
\dim Q_e=r-1,
\]

the edge support function has degree \(r-1\).  It is quadratic exactly when

\[
r=3.
\]

Only then does polarization recover the full support datum as a nondegenerate bilinear form.

### 4.2 Local half-dimension

The local incidence space at a cubic vertex has dimension

\[
3(r-1),
\]

while the diagonal family space has dimension \(r\).  It is half-dimensional exactly when

\[
2r=3(r-1),
\]

again giving \(r=3\).

### 4.3 Global index balance

For a cubic graph,

\[
|E|=\frac32|V|.
\]

The quotient-sheaf cochain dimensions balance exactly when

\[
r|V|=(r-1)|E|,
\]

which is the same equation

\[
2r=3(r-1).
\]

Thus rank three is simultaneously:

- the quadratic support rank;
- the local Lagrangian rank;
- the zero-index global rank.

This is the **rank-three coincidence principle**.

A parallel degree cutoff governs gauge-code parity: the nonzero indicator on \(\Gamma\) has degree \(r\), while graph bicycle identities control monomials through degree three.  Hence the general evenness theorem closes exactly for \(r\le3\), with rank four the first failure.

## 5. The all-rank hierarchy

The rank-three quadratic geometry disappears beyond rank three, but two broader structures survive.

### 5.1 Higher-degree Fano transgression

For a plane \(W\le\Gamma\) and \(D=W\setminus\{0\}\), let

\[
\nu_h:\Gamma/\langle h\rangle\to\mathbf F_2
\]

be the nonzero indicator.  Then for every \(x\in\Gamma\),

\[
\boxed{
\sum_{h\in D}\nu_h([x]_h)
=
\nu_{\Gamma/W}([x]_W).
}
\]

The left side has apparent degree \(r-1\); Fano summation cancels its top part and produces degree \(r-2\).  Thus Fano summation lowers support degree by one in every rank.

For a global quotient-sheaf section \(k\), this implies that the number of vertices with

\[
k_v\notin W_v
\]

is even.  At rank three, \(\Gamma/W_v\) is a line, so this scalar bit is the whole quotient coordinate.  At higher rank it forgets direction and is therefore insufficient for compatibility.

### 5.2 Dual-Fano residue

At a local flow plane \(W\), a legal dual configuration is a triple

\[
\eta_h\in\operatorname{Ann}(h),
\qquad
\sum_{h\in D}\eta_h=0.
\]

Let:

- \(eta_W(\eta)\) be the common cross-pairing bit;
- \(\sigma_W(\eta)\) be the parity of the nonzero components;
- \(ho_W(\eta)=1\) exactly when \(eta_W=0\) and all three components are nonzero.

When \(ho_W=1\), the three components are the three nonzero points of a plane

\[
P_\eta\le\operatorname{Ann}(W).
\]

Thus \(ho_W\) detects a hidden dual Fano line.  The exact local identity is

\[
\boxed{\sigma_W=\beta_W+\rho_W.}
\]

For an equilibrium stress \(\psi\), graph handshaking gives

\[
\sum_v\sigma_{W_v}(\psi_v)=0,
\]

while the affine obstruction pairing is

\[
\psi(c_f)=\sum_v\beta_{W_v}(\psi_v).
\]

Therefore

\[
\boxed{
\psi(c_f)=\sum_v\rho_{W_v}(\psi_v).
}
\]

This is the complete all-rank compatibility criterion:

\[
[c_f]=0
\iff
\text{every equilibrium stress has even dual-Fano residue parity}.
\]

### 5.3 Rank three: automatic vanishing

At rank three,

\[
\dim\operatorname{Ann}(W)=1.
\]

A line cannot contain a binary plane, so

\[
\rho_W\equiv0.
\]

This recovers automatic compatibility.  The old branching identity is precisely the no-residue statement.

### 5.4 Rank four: cubic/Pfaffian correction

At rank four,

\[
\dim\operatorname{Ann}(W)=2,
\]

so the annihilator itself is the unique possible hidden dual plane.  Legal configurations can be represented by alternating two-forms modulo

\[
\Lambda^2(\Gamma/W)^*.
\]

The residue becomes the cubic Boolean function

\[
\rho_W(\eta)
=
(1+\beta_W(\eta))\operatorname{Pf}(\alpha),
\]

where \(\alpha\) is any alternating-form representative.  Geometrically, the residue is one exactly when \(W\) is isotropic and the induced cross-pairing

\[
W\times\Gamma/W\to\mathbf F_2
\]

is perfect.

Thus rank four is the first post-quadratic layer, and the Pfaffian is the first correction term rather than an unrelated construction.

### 5.5 Minimal six-vertex comparison

The two connected simple cubic graphs on six vertices are the triangular prism and \(K_{3,3}\).  Their canonical spanning rank-four flows are both gauge-rigid and both have one-dimensional essential-stress space.  Nevertheless:

- the prism is compatible; its essential stress has no local residue;
- \(K_{3,3}\) is incompatible; a generator has exactly one residue vertex.

Therefore neither gauge rigidity nor obstruction-space dimension determines compatibility.  The decisive invariant is residue parity.

### 5.6 Symplectic parity anomaly

For a single global symplectic form on a rank-four coefficient space, the number of vertex flow planes that are Lagrangian is always even.  An essential stress, however, may be represented by locally compatible alternating forms that do not arise from one global form.  Their Lagrangian-plane parity may be odd.

Rank-four incompatibility can therefore be read as a symplectic parity anomaly: local symplectic data satisfy edge matching but fail global descent.

## 6. From incidence geometry to tensor and code homology

The tensor branch becomes coherent only after its exact relation to the incidence core is stated.

### 6.1 Flow tensor complex

Let

\[
Q_G=\mathbf F_2^E/\mathcal C(G)^\perp
\cong\mathcal C(G)^*.
\]

The flow induces a surjection

\[
F_f:Q_G\to\Gamma,
\qquad
F_f(\bar e)=f(e).
\]

Define

\[
T_f:\mathbf F_2^E\to\Gamma\otimes Q_G,
\qquad
T_f(\lambda)=\sum_e\lambda_ef(e)\otimes\bar e,
\]

and

\[
W_f:\Gamma\otimes Q_G\to\Lambda^2\Gamma,
\qquad
W_f(a\otimes q)=a\wedge F_f(q).
\]

Since \(f(e)\wedge f(e)=0\),

\[
W_fT_f=0.
\]

Thus

\[
\mathcal T_f:
\mathbf F_2^E
\xrightarrow{T_f}
\Gamma\otimes Q_G
\xrightarrow{W_f}
\Lambda^2\Gamma
\]

is a complex.

### 6.2 Exact chain-level bridge

Let

\[
D_G:\Gamma^V\to\Gamma^E
\]

be graph coboundary,

\[
J_f:\mathbf F_2^E\to\Gamma^E,
\qquad
(J_f\lambda)_e=\lambda_ef(e),
\]

and

\[
P_G:\Gamma^E\to\Gamma\otimes Q_G,
\qquad
P_G(g)=\sum_eg_e\otimes\bar e.
\]

The universal tension resolution is exact:

\[
0\to\Gamma\to\Gamma^V
\xrightarrow{D_G}\Gamma^E
\xrightarrow{P_G}\Gamma\otimes Q_G\to0.
\]

The lifted incidence complex

\[
\widehat{\mathcal R}_f:
\Gamma^V\oplus\mathbf F_2^E
\xrightarrow{D_G+J_f}
\Gamma^E
\longrightarrow
\Lambda^2\Gamma
\]

maps in two directions:

\[
\widehat{\mathscr Q}_f
\xleftarrow{\simeq}
\widehat{\mathcal R}_f
\twoheadrightarrow
\mathcal T_f.
\]

The left arrow removes a contractible flow-line lift; the right arrow quotients the universal tension resolution.  This is the precise chain-level meaning of “tensor reduction”.

Consequently:

\[
0\to\Gamma\to H^0(G;\mathscr Q_f)
\to H_0(\mathcal T_f)\to0,
\]

and

\[
H_1(\mathcal T_f)
\cong
\ker\bigl(H^1(G;\mathscr Q_f)\to\Lambda^2\Gamma\bigr).
\]

The affine class already has zero wedge moment, so it lies canonically in tensor middle homology.  No arbitrary projection is involved.

### 6.3 Homology dictionary

Define

\[
\mathcal B_f=\ker T_f,
\qquad
\mathcal K_f=\ker W_f/\operatorname{im}T_f.
\]

Then:

- \(\mathcal B_f\cong H^0(G;\mathscr Q_f)/\Gamma\) is the reduced gauge-moduli code;
- \(\mathcal K_f^*\cong\operatorname{Stress}(f)/\operatorname{AltStress}(f)\) is the essential-stress space;
- the compatibility class is a distinguished element \(\kappa_f\in\mathcal K_f\).

At rank three, Lagrangian self-duality gives

\[
\mathcal B_f\cong\mathcal K_f^*.
\]

This is the reduced Fano Hodge theorem.

### 6.4 Nested code flags

Dualizing the based quotient datum gives a nested pair of binary codes

\[
D\subseteq C\subseteq\mathbf F_2^E.
\]

For a graph,

\[
D=\mathcal F_f
\subseteq
C=\mathcal C(G),
\]

where \(\mathcal F_f\) is the coordinate-flow code.

The dual tensor complex is

\[
\Lambda^2D
\xrightarrow{\kappa}
D\otimes C
\xrightarrow{\mu}
\mathbf F_2^E,
\qquad
\mu(d\otimes c)=d*c.
\]

Thus:

- the row code is the Schur product \(C*D\);
- the gauge code is \((C*D)^\perp\);
- essential stresses are first syzygies of Schur multiplication modulo universal commutativity relations;
- the column matroid of \(T_f\) is the flow constraint matroid.

The term “mixed Schur–Koszul complex” is descriptive and provisional until the literature-recognition problem is settled.

### 6.5 Divided-square exact sequence

For a surjection \(F:Q\to\Gamma\) with kernel \(K\), define

\[
W(a\otimes q)=a\wedge F(q).
\]

There is a canonical exact sequence

\[
0\to\Gamma\otimes K
\to\ker W
\to D^2\Gamma
\to0,
\]

where

\[
D^2\Gamma=\ker(\Gamma\otimes\Gamma\to\Lambda^2\Gamma).
\]

The edge tensor maps to

\[
f(e)\otimes f(e),
\]

the characteristic-two Veronese column.  In rank three, \(\dim D^2\Gamma=6\), explaining the six exceptional labels in the basis-packing determinant.  The coordinate basis-packing formula is a determinant-line expansion of this exact sequence, not the invariant definition.

### 6.6 Index, balancedness, and torsion

For a based quotient datum \(U_E\to Q\to\Gamma\), the tensor index is

\[
\operatorname{ind}
=|E|-\dim\Gamma\,\dim Q+
\binom{\dim\Gamma}{2}.
\]

A balanced datum has index zero.  In this case the top wedge

\[
\Theta_f=\bigwedge_{e\in E}
(f(e)\otimes\bar e)
\in\det(\ker W_f)
\]

is nonzero exactly when the complex is exact.  For cubic rank-three flows this binary Fano torsion is one exactly when the flow is gauge-rigid.

Balancedness, Hodge self-duality, and vanishing of the affine class are logically distinct:

1. balancedness is an Euler/dimension statement;
2. Hodge duality identifies flexibility and obstruction spaces;
3. the Fano compatibility theorem kills the distinguished affine obstruction.

Conflating these three produced several earlier “master object” formulations.

### 6.7 Functorial branches

Coefficient quotients \(\Gamma\twoheadrightarrow\Gamma'\) induce ordinary chain maps and a relative wedge complex, hence a long exact sequence relating gauge and obstruction homology.  The rank-two moduli count appears as a controlled degeneration of rank three.

Strict morphisms of based quotient data are coordinate set maps preserving the two levels of the dual code flag.  Graph deletion, contraction, and cut gluing have mixed variance and generally require spans or linear correspondences rather than ordinary maps.

## 7. Graphical rigidity, circuits, and interfaces

This branch studies the homogeneous space \(\mathcal B_f\), not the affine existence theorem.

### 7.1 Harmonic cut quotients

For \(\lambda\in\mathcal B_f\), choose a vertex potential \(k\) with

\[
k_u+k_v=\lambda_ef(e).
\]

On every component of \(G-\operatorname{supp}\lambda\), the potential is constant.  Contract these components.  The support edges form a quotient multigraph \(Q\) on which the restricted labels are simultaneously:

- a nowhere-zero flow;
- an exact tension.

Thus every nonzero gauge word is a nowhere-zero flow–tension bicycle on a connected cut quotient, and conversely.  Minimal gauge supports are precisely support-minimal such quotients, i.e. circuits of the constraint matroid.

This unifies three languages:

\[
\text{tensor dependency}
\Longleftrightarrow
\text{gauge word}
\Longleftrightarrow
\text{harmonic cut quotient}.
\]

### 7.2 Evenness for rank at most three

Coordinates of a flow–tension bicycle lie in the binary bicycle space.  Graph integration by parts annihilates all squarefree monomials of degrees one, two, and three.  The nonzero indicator on \(\mathbf F_2^3\) has degree three, so every gauge word has even weight when

\[
\dim\Gamma\le3.
\]

The result is sharp: rank four admits an odd all-edge gauge word on a suitably labelled \(K_6\).

Consequences include:

- the constraint matroid has only even circuits in rank at most three;
- no odd-weight harmonic cut quotient occurs;
- the Schur-code identity
  
  \[
  \mathbf1_E\in\mathcal C(G)*\mathcal F_f
  \]
  
  holds.

### 7.3 Low-weight circuits

The harmonic quotient viewpoint gives:

- weight one: impossible;
- weight two: an equal-labelled two-edge bond;
- weight three: impossible;
- weight four: an equal-labelled four-edge bond;
- weight five: impossible by evenness;
- weight six: exactly \(6K_2\), doubled \(K_3\), or an affine-plane-labelled \(K_4\).

The next unresolved circuit layer is weight eight.

### 7.4 Interface sewing

Cut a graph along boundary edges \(S\) and cap the two sides.  The cycle space is a fiber product over the even boundary-pattern space; the cographic quotient is the dual pushout.  Edge coordinates and cographic quotients therefore have opposite variance.

Let

\[
L_S=\bigcap_{e\in S}\langle f(e)\rangle.
\]

For interfaces with the cap-extension property, the pure sewing modes form \(L_S\), while matching obstructions live in \(\Gamma/L_S\):

\[
0\to L_S\to\Gamma\to\Gamma/L_S\to0.
\]

Equivalently, \(L_S\) is the gauge space of the labelled theta interface.  The reduced moduli dimensions satisfy

\[
b_G=b_A+b_B+\dim L_S.
\]

For a two-edge cut, the labels are equal, \(\dim L_S=1\), and one sewing bit appears.  For a nowhere-zero three-edge cut, the labels are distinct and \(L_S=0\); there is no sewing bit.

Therefore:

\[
b_G=b_A+b_B+1
\quad\text{for a two-edge sum},
\]

\[
b_G=b_A+b_B
\quad\text{for a three-edge sum}.
\]

Compatible-surface polynomials and Fano torsion factor accordingly:

\[
\Pi_G=2\Pi_A\Pi_B,
\qquad
\tau_G=0
\]

for two-edge gluing, while

\[
\Pi_G=\Pi_A\Pi_B,
\qquad
\tau_G=\tau_A\tau_B
\]

for three-edge gluing.

Cut decomposition and circuit contraction are the same local interface theorem viewed in opposite directions.

## 8. Embedding and surface branch

The archived 2026-07-14 research layer contains a second downstream program.  Its common idea is that compatible affine/gauge states can be interpreted as rotation-system or partial-Petrial data, producing embedded surfaces and transition structures.

The substantive components named by the snapshot and retained in the working synthesis are:

1. **Gauge–embedding correspondence.**  Gauge choices index compatible local rotations or embedding modifications.
2. **Partial Petrial surface census.**  Enumerates surfaces obtained by selected edge twists and records orientability/genus data.
3. **Orientation exact sequence.**  Separates gauge freedom from orientability constraints.
4. **Syndrome/transition invariant.**  Packages local transition choices and their global parity or syndrome.
5. **Transition-matroid code slice.**  Relates compatible states to a slice of transition or delta-matroid data.
6. **Code-filtered Petrial polynomial.**  A state sum restricted by the gauge code.
7. **Cut factorization.**  Two-edge and three-edge interface laws for state counts and compatible-surface polynomials.
8. **Computational evidence.**  Surface censuses, moduli counts, transition checks, and scripts listed in the snapshot manifest.

This material is not discarded.  Its present status is nevertheless different from Sections 2–7:

- the source bodies are not present in the current `main` tree, only their hash commitments and filenames;
- some conclusions are computational census statements rather than proofs;
- the exact dependence on the affine incidence core versus the homogeneous gauge code must be restated;
- delta-matroid, transition-polynomial, and Petrial terminology requires a real external literature comparison.

Accordingly, this branch is preserved as a named program and publication candidate, but it must not be used as support for the core theorem until its sources are rematerialized and audited.

## 10. Canonicalization and deduplication decisions

The active dossier collection contains repeated discoveries at increasing levels of invariant clarity.  The following rules prevent wheel-spinning prose from becoming parallel ontologies.

### 10.1 Incidence pair over tensor “master object”

The affine incidence-pair complex is the centre of compatibility.  The tensor complex is a canonical chain-level reduction and a broader code-theoretic generalization.  The old “master object” title is retained only as provenance.

### 10.2 Local package over separate case splits

The Fano quadratic-Lagrangian package is the canonical local theorem.  The quadratic transgression is its key identity; cross-bit, support parity, annihilator uniqueness, and branching tables are projections or lemmas, not separate foundational mechanisms.

### 10.3 Global intersection over multiple cancellation narratives

Affine Lagrangian intersection is the canonical global theorem.  Stress cancellation, quadratic handshaking, support-boundary cancellation, and Fano Hodge duality remain valuable proof views and translations, but should not be narrated as four unrelated reasons for compatibility.

### 10.4 All-rank residue over isolated rank-four obstruction

The dual-Fano residue theorem controls the hierarchy.  The rank-four Pfaffian formula is its first nontrivial algebraic specialization.  The \(K_{3,3}\) calculation is the minimal negative example; the six-vertex dichotomy is the cleaner comparative statement.

### 10.5 Chain bridge over vague compression language

The chain-level incidence–tensor zigzag supersedes the phrase “reduced compression”.  The earlier cohomological comparison remains useful for its short exact sequences and explicit maps.

### 10.6 Harmonic quotients over separate low-cut observations

Gauge circuits, equal-labelled two-edge bonds, absence of three-edge sewing, and cut-factorization laws are manifestations of one harmonic-interface geometry.

### 10.7 History and computation are evidence, not premises

Research logs, handoffs, scripts, and censuses belong to provenance or verification.  A mathematical chapter may cite the resulting theorem or computation, but should not reproduce the conversational path by which it was found.

## 12. Reliability boundary

The original AffineCDC implementation in the companion Lean repository remains the machine-checked anchor.  Many invariant formulations in this directory are theorem-level drafts written after that implementation.  A cleaner proof is not automatically a verified proof of the same formal statement.

Before publication, each natural block must therefore carry an explicit correspondence table:

1. formal source definitions;
2. manuscript definitions;
3. theorem statement equivalence;
4. proof obligations introduced by compression;
5. finite computations used only as examples or audits.

The repository should prefer a smaller number of audited canonical theorems over a larger number of synonymous structural slogans.

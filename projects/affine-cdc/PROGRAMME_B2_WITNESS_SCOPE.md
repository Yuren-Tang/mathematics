# Programme B2 witness-scope boundaries

## 1. Full graph-level witnesses

Each of the following retains enough data to reconstruct five indexed even supports on a finite loopless cubic multigraph:

- five indexed supports;
- an $R_5$-valued flow;
- a $K_5$-triangle labelling;
- an anisotropic $O^-(4,2)$ flow;
- cycle words $(b,d,x,y)$ satisfying the quadratic equation;
- a cycle-continuous edge map to $M^*(K_5)$ under the exact inverse-cycle definition.

These are mutually reconstructible and belong in one graph-level equivalence box.

## 2. Fixed-data solvability criteria

The following become exact equivalences only after the fixed quotient data are retained:

- singular-line lifting;
- the component lift defect;
- fixed-plane colour-cut parity;
- existence of the eliminated cycle word $d$;
- the Schur condition $x*y\in\mathcal C(Q_b)$.

The required retained data include the quotient flow, kernel line or plane, quotient graph, and lift torsor. A quotient residue or boundary word alone is not a source witness.

## 3. Linear dual criteria

Relative stresses are the orthogonal complement of the attainable evaluation code. They give an exact Fredholm alternative for one prescribed target.

A stress space alone does not contain:

- the fixed flow;
- the base lift;
- the prescribed target;
- a primal preimage;
- the reconstructed root flow.

It is a dual dependency space, not a graph-level witness.

## 4. Fourier counting criteria

The Fourier formula exactly counts the intersection of one fixed affine evaluation code with one fixed allowed set.

- positive count: existence in that fixed orbit;
- explicit witness: still requires a codeword and primal preimage;
- zero count: aggregate spectral cancellation;
- single separating stress: an additional property, not an automatic consequence of zero count.

Finite enumerators and phase tables remain finite results unless a uniform estimate is proved separately.

## 5. Orthogonal target models

The five-support mother space $O^-(4,2)$ and the eight-support exceptional space $O^+(6,2)$ remain valid.

The proposed universal $O^+(2r,2)$ hierarchy is false for $r\ge4$. The correct universal additive complete-graph root module has dimension $q-2$, not $2r$, for $q=2^r$.

The superseded packet may be cited only as historical provenance of the failed claim and the question it exposed.

## 6. Quantifier rule

A graph-level equivalence quantifies existentially over all auxiliary flow, coordinate, quotient, and lift data unless those data are explicitly fixed.

A successful fixed-data criterion implies a graph-level witness. Its converse at the same fixed data is asserted only when an explicit reconstruction theorem is present.

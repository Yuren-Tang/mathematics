# AffineCDC

This directory studies one mathematical mechanism and the structures generated by it.

> A nowhere-zero binary flow on a cubic graph determines local affine families.  In rank three, the local Fano geometry forces these families to admit a globally compatible choice; the compatible choice then yields a cycle double cover.

The project contains much more than that proof, but the additional material is not all at the same logical level.  The active organization is therefore by mathematical dependency, not by date of discovery or by the file in which an idea first appeared.

## The canonical spine

Let \(G\) be a finite cubic graph, let \(\Gamma\) be a binary vector space, and let

\[
f:E(G)\to\Gamma
\]

be a nowhere-zero flow.  For each edge put

\[
Q_e=\Gamma/\langle f(e)\rangle.
\]

The incidence labels form a space \(E_f\).  Local homogeneous families form a subspace \(L_{\mathrm{vert}}\), edge matching forms a subspace \(L_{\mathrm{edge}}\), and the actual local affine families form a coset

\[
\kappa+L_{\mathrm{vert}}.
\]

The central object is the affine incidence-pair complex

\[
\mathcal P_f:
L_{\mathrm{vert}}\oplus L_{\mathrm{edge}}
\xrightarrow{+}
E_f,
\qquad
\kappa\in E_f.
\]

It records simultaneously:

\[
H^0(\mathcal P_f)=L_{\mathrm{vert}}\cap L_{\mathrm{edge}},
\]

\[
H^1(\mathcal P_f)=E_f/(L_{\mathrm{vert}}+L_{\mathrm{edge}}),
\]

and the compatibility obstruction

\[
[\kappa]\in H^1(\mathcal P_f).
\]

Compatibility means \([\kappa]=0\).  If compatible, the solution set is a torsor under \(H^0(\mathcal P_f)\).

In rank three, every \(Q_e\) is a binary plane with a canonical anisotropic quadratic form.  The vertex space becomes a Lagrangian, the local affine coset becomes its characteristic torsor, and the edge-matching space becomes a totally singular Lagrangian.  The abstract affine Lagrangian-intersection theorem then forces

\[
[\kappa]=0.
\]

This is the proof-theoretic centre of AffineCDC.

## What is core, and what is downstream

### Core compatibility geometry

- local affine classification;
- the affine incidence-pair complex;
- quotient-sheaf and stress presentations;
- the rank-three Fano quadratic-Lagrangian enhancement;
- compatibility, its full solution torsor, and CDC extraction.

### Rank hierarchy and obstruction theory

- the all-rank support transgression;
- the rank-three coincidence principle;
- the dual-Fano residue criterion in every rank;
- the rank-four cubic/Pfaffian residue;
- the triangular-prism/\(K_{3,3}\) six-vertex dichotomy;
- symplectic parity anomalies.

These results explain both the positive rank-three theorem and the first genuine failures beyond it.

### Reduced tensor and code theory

The quotient sheaf admits a canonical chain-level reduction to the flow tensor complex.  Its degree-zero homology is the reduced gauge space; its middle homology is the zero-wedge-moment obstruction space.  Dualizing yields a nested code flag and a mixed Schur–Koszul-type complex.  This branch contains:

- tensor homology and index;
- constraint matroids;
- divided-square structure;
- coefficient-quotient exact sequences;
- morphisms and automorphisms;
- Fano torsion.

This theory is genuinely related to the core, but it is a reduction and generalization of the incidence geometry, not the primitive compatibility object.

### Graphical rigidity and decomposition

The homogeneous solution space has its own graph geometry:

- gauge modes are harmonic cut quotients;
- the rank-at-most-three gauge code is even;
- low-weight circuits admit concrete quotient classifications;
- cut gluing is governed by labelled theta interfaces;
- two-edge and three-edge sums satisfy exact moduli and torsion laws.

These are downstream theorems about flexibility and decomposition.  They do not enter the existence proof of the compatible affine family.

### Embedding, Petrial, transition, and surface material

The archived research layer also contains gauge–embedding correspondences, partial Petrials, orientability sequences, transition-matroid slices, surface censuses, and state-sum polynomials.  They are retained as a topological/computational program, but are not currently part of the theorem spine.  The 2026-07-14 manifest in [`snapshots/`](snapshots/) commits the original source files and computations by hash; the source bodies must be rematerialized before this branch can be audited and rewritten at theorem level.

## Status discipline

The directory deliberately separates four kinds of assertion:

- **Lean-verified implementation** — currently supplied by the companion `Yuren-Tang/affine-cdc` repository;
- **theorem-level manuscript draft** — a complete mathematical argument is written, but the present compression has not necessarily been independently audited or formalized;
- **exact finite computation** — a finite case or identity has been exhaustively checked, but is not a general proof;
- **programmatic or conjectural structure** — a proposed framework, recognition problem, or topological extension.

No theorem dossier should be promoted merely because its vocabulary is general.  In particular, “flow tensor datum as the master object” is an historical title: the affine incidence-pair complex is the compatibility centre; the tensor datum is its reduced algebraic shadow and a possible independent generalization.

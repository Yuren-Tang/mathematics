## 2. Response geometry

For the route-capped carrier, a cocycle word is

$$
(z,a,z+a),
$$

and

$$
a\cdot z'=\mathcal B(z,z').
$$

The form `\mathcal B` is the mod-two intersection form of the associated signed ribbon surface. Its radical is boundary homology and its rank is Euler genus. A partial Petrial `\lambda` adds

$$
\sum_e\lambda_ez_ez'_e.
$$

## 3. Gauge-rigid certificate

Let `C` be the admissible Petrial code. A response cycle `z` fails to become radical under all admissible gauges exactly when there exists a physical cycle `z'` such that

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal B(z,z')=1,
\qquad
w=z\odot z'\in C^\perp.
}
$$

For the Fano-flow gauge code,

$$
C^\perp=\mathcal C(G)*\mathcal F_f.
$$

Thus

$$
\boxed{w_e=\langle u(e),f(e)\rangle}
$$

for a dual-valued flow `u:E\to\Gamma^*`. The word `w` is the quadratic norm support of the hyperbolic flow `(f,u)`.

## 4. Dual rank one

If `u(E)` spans one dimension, then

$$
w=c*(\alpha\circ f).
$$

The support on every scalar circuit is empty, full, or an alternating union of marked intervals. The odd intersection decomposes over support components, so one component is odd.

Hence rank one reduces to

$$
\boxed{
\text{one odd scalar circuit}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\text{one odd marked scalar interval}.
}
$$

The remaining rank-one problem is composition and root-admissibility.

## 5. Dual rank two

Let the image plane of `u` have annihilator colour `h`. The quotient fixed flow and the dual flow are two four-flows.

At a fully Tait vertex their three nonzero values define a permutation `\sigma_v\in S_3`; the norm support is its moved-point set. Since an intersection of two cubic cycles cannot use all three incident edges, `\sigma_v` is identity or a transposition, never a three-cycle.

Therefore path endpoints of `w` lie only at:

- the Fano colour matching `E_h`;
- the zero-value locus of the auxiliary four-flow.

One component carries the odd intersection. Hence rank two reduces to

$$
\boxed{
\text{one odd closed norm circuit}
\quad\text{or}\quad
\text{one odd defect-to-defect path}.
}
$$

The remaining rank-two problem is composition through finite endpoint semantics.

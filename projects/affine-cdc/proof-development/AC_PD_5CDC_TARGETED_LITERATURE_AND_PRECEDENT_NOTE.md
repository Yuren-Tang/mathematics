## 1. Five-cycle-double-cover literature

### Classical and recent special-class results

- A. Huck and M. Kochol, **Five cycle double covers of some cubic graphs**, *Journal of Combinatorial Theory, Series B* 64 (1995), 119–125.
- A. Huck, **On cycle-double covers of graphs with small oddness**, *Discrete Mathematics* 229 (2001), 125–165.
- A. Hoffmann-Ostenhof, **A note on 5-cycle double covers**, arXiv:1209.0096 (2012). Gives a necessary and sufficient condition for a prescribed 2-regular subgraph to lie in a 5-CDC; it does not prove universal existence.
- S. Liu, R.-X. Hao, R. Luo, C.-Q. Zhang, **5-Cycle Double Covers, 4-Flows, and Catlin Reduction**, *SIAM Journal on Discrete Mathematics* 37 (2023), 253–267, DOI `10.1137/22M1472425`. Proves oddness-at-most-four and several superposition/snark families via 4-flows and Catlin reduction.
- S. Liu et al., **Five-cycle double cover and shortest cycle cover**, *Journal of Graph Theory* 108 (2025), DOI `10.1002/jgt.23164`. Gives further sufficient conditions and classes.
- **Non-separating cycles and 5-cycle double covers**, *Discrete Mathematics* (2025), article `S0012365X25001232`. Gives additional sufficient conditions involving a prescribed non-separating cycle.

The recent peer-reviewed literature still treats universal 5-CDC as a conjecture and proves special classes or sufficient conditions. None of the located results contains the present one-cross relative edge-extension theorem, the ten-state root boundary calculus, or the singular coloured-weld return mechanism.

### Relevance to the present proof

The literature supports the standard reductions to cubic/snark-like cores and offers useful comparison with 4-flow and Catlin-reduction methods. It does not currently replace either:

1. `R1`, the one-cross structural reducibility theorem; or
2. `R2`, cover-independent relative extension across the deleted two-vertex cap.

The current route should therefore be presented as a new proof mechanism if it is completed, subject to a later independent novelty audit.

## 2. Coloured Pachner and balanced-complex precedents

### Color-preserving bistellar connectivity

I. Izmestiev, S. Klee, I. Novik, **Simplicial moves on balanced complexes**, arXiv:1512.04384.

They prove, among other results, that two properly `m`-coloured triangulations of the same closed combinatorial `d`-manifold are connected by colour-preserving bistellar flips when `m>=d+2`; balanced triangulations are connected by cross-flips.

This theorem does not prove `R2`: both endpoint triangulations are assumed already properly coloured. In the five-support problem the target source topology is precisely the endpoint whose proper five-colouring/root flow is unknown.

### Weld obstruction on surfaces

S. Murai, Y. Suzuki, **Balanced subdivisions and flips on surfaces**, arXiv:1701.08060.

They show that balanced stellar subdivisions and welds alone do not necessarily connect balanced triangulations of a closed surface; an additional pentagon contraction is needed in their connectivity theorem.

This is not a direct theorem about the present root-triality system, but it confirms that reversing coloured subdivisions/welds is a substantive problem. One cannot regard the inverse equal-face weld as formal after obtaining a coloured refinement.

### Projectivity

M. Joswig, **Projectivities in Simplicial Complexes and Colorings of Simple Polytopes**, arXiv:math/0102186.

Joswig's projectivity group records vertex-identification transport along facet paths and controls colouring obstructions. The forced Petersen transport and cap-oriented resolution sheet have a closely analogous form. At present this is an interpretation, not a proved identification with Joswig's group, and no general projectivity theorem located in the literature supplies the missing relative inverse-weld return.

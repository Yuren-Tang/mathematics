## 0. Disposition

Two later BBD synthesis claims are not justified by the mature preceding theorem spine.

1. Individual physical lifted loops do not automatically generate one root-preserving affine group. The missing transport/composability theorem is load-bearing for the claimed vanishing translation kernel and simultaneous common origin.
2. Minimizing non-root edges over all $E_5$-flows with the original terminal roots is trivial, because the original indexed root cover itself belongs to that affine space and has zero defects. The stated variational forest cannot describe a nontrivial residual canonical flow on that domain.

The algebraic cohomology, $K_6$ coefficient geometry, local defect-strip grammar, and Petersen transport may be retained with exact conditional scopes. The global BBD common-flow and residual forest chain must not remain unconditional.

## 1. What one physical loop gives

Let $x$ be one indexed root cover of a four-pole and let a genuine lifted boundary loop $\gamma$ end at $x^\gamma$ with boundary support permutation $\pi_\gamma$. Then

$$
z_\gamma=x^\gamma+\pi_\gamma x
\in M_P:=Z_1(P)\otimes E_5.
$$

The pair

$$
A_\gamma=(\pi_\gamma,z_\gamma)
$$

defines an affine transformation of the **ambient** torsor $M_P$:

$$
u\longmapsto\pi_\gamma u+z_\gamma.
$$

B6.1 classifies one such transformation, under a repeatability hypothesis, by its cyclic norm. None of this says that $A_\gamma$ preserves the nonlinear root-valued subset at arbitrary ambient points.

## 2. Why algebraic generation is insufficient

Let

$$
\mathcal H_{\mathrm{alg}}
=\langle A_\gamma:\gamma\text{ one physical based boundary loop at }x\rangle
\le M_P\rtimes S_5
$$

be the abstract affine group generated by the individual ambient transformations. This group exists algebraically.

The BBD translation-kernel proof requires:

$$
\boxed{
A(x)\text{ is root-valued for every }A\in\mathcal H_{\mathrm{alg}}.}
$$

That assertion has not been proved.

A path switch is root-preserving at the state where its selected edge set is a component of the relevant support-pair symmetric difference. After another lifted loop, the same fixed edge set need not remain such a component; toggling it can create edge words of weight zero or four. Therefore an individual physical loop need not define a root-preserving transformation on the whole ambient torsor, and products of the ambient transformations need not correspond to composable physical reconfiguration walks.

## 3. Exact missing groupoid theorem

To recover the BBD synthesis, one needs a theorem of the following form.

### Required theorem — physical BBD isotropy closure

Construct an actual reconfiguration groupoid $\mathscr G_{\mathrm{BBD}}$ whose objects are indexed root-valued covers with the fixed terminal data and whose morphisms are physical lifted BBD paths. Prove:

1. every one of the sixteen boundary monodromies has a morphism from one common base object $x$;
2. each such morphism may be transported to the endpoint of every other physical path needed for composition;
3. transported paths remain genuine support-pair component switches;
4. inverses are physical;
5. the isotropy/transport closure acts affinely on $M_P$ with the displayed semidirect-product law;
6. every pure translation in its kernel sends $x$ to a root-valued cover;
7. its boundary projection is the full $S_5$.

Only after these points are proved may root rigidity be applied to the translation kernel.

This is not a formal bookkeeping lemma: it is a theorem about correlated path components across the physical lift family. The preceding local-to-global packet explicitly identifies family correlation as open.

## 4. Conditional root-rigidity theorem

Assume the required groupoid theorem and let

$$
\mathcal H\le M_P\rtimes S_5
$$

be the resulting root-preserving affine group, surjecting onto $S_5$. Let

$$
T=\ker(\mathcal H\to S_5).
$$

Then the root-rigidity proof is valid.

### Theorem 4.1 — conditional translation-kernel elimination

Under the root-preserving group-action hypothesis,

$$
\boxed{T=0.}
$$

### Proof

For each edge $e$, evaluation gives an $S_5$-stable subspace

$$
T_e=\{v(e):v\in T\}\le E_5.
$$

The natural even module $E_5$ is irreducible under $S_5$: a nonzero root orbit spans it, and the difference of two co-roots is a root. Hence $T_e$ is $0$ or $E_5$.

Root preservation gives

$$
x(e)+T_e\subseteq R_5.
$$

The case $T_e=E_5$ is impossible because the coset is all of $E_5$, containing zero and co-roots. Thus every $T_e=0$, so $T=0$. $\square$

The proof is sound; its group-action hypothesis is the missing point.

## 5. Conditional simultaneous origin

Once $T=0$, the physical affine group is the graph of one cocycle

$$
c:S_5\to M_P.
$$

The algebraic theorem

$$
H^1(S_5,E_5)=0
$$

is valid. Since five is odd,

$$
\mathbf F_2^5=\mathbf1\oplus E_5,
$$

and Shapiro's lemma applied to the five-point permutation module gives the vanishing. Hence

$$
H^1(S_5,M_P)=0.
$$

Therefore, **conditional on the physical group theorem**, there is a unique common ambient origin $y$ such that

$$
z_\pi=(1+\pi)y
$$

for all physical BBD holonomies. The shifted flow

$$
r=x+y
$$

is then well defined.

Without the groupoid closure, there is no single cocycle on $S_5$ to which this cohomology theorem can be applied.

## 6. Valid coefficient geometry conditional on a common flow

For any $E_5$-valued flow $r$, independently of its origin:

1. the fifteen nonzero vectors of $E_5$ are canonically the fifteen edges of $K_6$;
2. roots are the ten edges inside the distinguished $K_5$;
3. co-roots are the five edges incident with $\infty$;
4. three distinct nonzero values sum to zero exactly when the $K_6$ edges form a triangle or a perfect matching;
5. if $r$ has no zero edge, its co-root subgraph has degrees $0,2,1$ according to root-triangle, triangle-through-$\infty$, or one-factor local type, and is therefore a union of internal paths and cycles;
6. zero edges are equality wires with local patterns $(0,0,0)$, $(0,2,2)$, or $(0,4,4)$.

These are valid local theorems. They become a canonical BBD residual geometry only if the common shifted flow $r$ is first justified.

## 7. The defect-minimization domain error

The forest packet defines

$$
\mathcal A
=
\{\text{$E_5$-flows with the same terminal roots as the original cover}\}
$$

and minimizes

$$
\delta(r)=|\{e:r(e)\notin R_5\}|.
$$

But the original indexed cover $x$ is itself an $R_5$-valued flow with those terminal roots. Therefore

$$
\boxed{
\min_{r\in\mathcal A}\delta(r)=0.}
$$

Every true minimizer may be chosen root-valued, and its defect support is empty. The cycle inequalities and forest statement are then formally true but vacuous. They do not refine the non-root canonical BBD flow.

## 8. Why the obvious repair does not work automatically

One might try to minimize only among flows that simultaneously linearize the BBD holonomies. Under the claimed $S_5$ common-origin theorem, the common origin is unique because

$$
M_P^{S_5}=0.
$$

Thus the holonomy-compatible slice is a singleton, not an affine space stable under arbitrary cycle toggles. The cycle toggles used in the forest proof need not preserve the simultaneous-origin equations.

Therefore the published variational proof cannot simply be restricted to the canonical slice.

A nontrivial forest theorem would require a newly defined admissible variation space $L\le M_P$ such that:

1. $r+L$ preserves the precise global BBD constraints one intends to retain;
2. $L$ contains enough cycle toggles for the averaging inequalities;
3. $x$ is not an automatic zero-defect competitor unless it satisfies those constraints;
4. the resulting minimizer remains linked to physical reconfiguration.

No such space is presently proved.

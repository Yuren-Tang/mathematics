## 1. Fixed marked-surface layer

Let

\[
x:E(G)\to R_5
\]

be one root-valued cubic flow. Its root-triangle pseudocomplex

\[
\mathfrak T_x
\]

has one support-labelled triangle per source vertex and one glued side per source edge.

Split every singular complex vertex according to the connected components of its link. This produces a compact marked surface, possibly disconnected or nonorientable, without changing:

- the dual cubic graph;
- any triangle;
- any side label;
- any terminal incidence;
- any root-flow equation.

Two adjacent distinct root triangles form the two faces of one labelled tetrahedron. Their unique nontrivial root NNI is exactly the diagonal flip replacing one diagonal of the corresponding ordinary quadrilateral by the other.

### Proposition 1.1 — same-order surface invariance

Inside a prime same-order block, every allowed root `2--2` move is a diagonal flip on the same underlying marked surface.

### Proof

A diagonal flip replaces two triangles filling one embedded quadrilateral by the other two triangles filling the same quadrilateral. It changes neither the ambient topological surface nor its marked boundary incidences. Repeating ordinary flips therefore remains in one connected component of the triangulation groupoid of that marked surface.

If a proposed local move is not an ordinary embedded quadrilateral after link splitting, the root-triangle classification places it in one of:

- equal-face `2--0` cancellation;
- self-folded or repeated-side degeneration;
- loop/parallel neighbourhood;
- two-/three-/four-edge separator;
- bounded appendage.

Those are strict-order or category exits and are not part of the prime same-order block. ∎

Thus the standard Ptolemy-groupoid presentation applies exactly to the underlying uncoloured same-order histories.

## 5. Complete history-space tube of an even track

`PTOLEMY_EVEN_TRACK_ROOT_TUBE_FILLING_V1.md` closes the corrected even-cycle problem without any four-pole compression.

Let

\[
\gamma
\]

be one embedded closed defect track in a lifted Ptolemy van Kampen disk. Since the ambient diagram is a disk, a sufficiently small regular neighbourhood

\[
N(\gamma)
\]

is an annulus.

The dual of this annulus is a cycle multipole:

- one cubic vertex for every turn of the track;
- one longitudinal edge between consecutive turns;
- one side semiedge at each turn carrying the actual emitted side root `z_i`.

All exterior components meet the tube only through these side incidences.

A root tube filling is a cyclic root sequence

\[
x_0,\ldots,x_{n-1}\in R_5
\]

satisfying

\[
\boxed{x_{i-1}+x_i=z_i.}
\]

It changes only the longitudinal labels and leaves every side root and exterior attachment untouched.

### Theorem 5.1 — explicit even-track fillings

For every `C6` side word

\[
(a,b,c,a,b,c),
\qquad a+b+c=0,
\]

the longitudinal sequence

\[
(b,c,a,b,c,a)
\]

is a root filling.

For the representative `C8` side word

\[
(24,34,23,12,13,34,14,12),
\]

the longitudinal sequence

\[
(25,45,35,25,15,35,45,15)
\]

is a root filling. `S_5` covariance covers every `C8` orbit member.

### Theorem 5.2 — complete closed-track disposition

Every closed persistent multi-cell first-failure track is either:

1. `C5` or `C9`, hence forbidden by oriented resolution parity;
2. `C6` or `C8`, hence removable inside its annular history-space neighbourhood while preserving every exterior root, side attachment, ordered boundary state, and cap route.

No enclosure into a four-pole is required.

## 6. Correct same-order conclusion

Consider a same-order contextual state graph for one fixed cap-target order and fixed outer route.

If a recurrent nonterminal component existed, a persistent co-root track would contain a simple Petersen cycle after deleting constant runs and backtracks.

- Odd cycles cannot close.
- Even cycles can be removed from the lifted history while retaining the same exterior enriched state and strictly reducing the number of defect-track cells.

Choose a recurrent representative with minimal defect-track complexity. Either alternative contradicts recurrence/minimality.

### Theorem 6.1 — authoritative no-sink theorem

The same-order contextual state graph has no nonterminal sink strongly connected component.

This theorem is justified by the forced-backbone reduction plus explicit annular tube filling, not by direct application of equality/DDD four-pole potentials to the cycle core.

### Corollary 6.2 — same-order progress

From every same-order contextual state, some finite allowed schedule reaches one of:

1. complete root-valued inverse transfer;
2. route/profile cap escape;
3. separator/category exit;
4. bounded direct-pairing completion;
5. a stage lying behind an actual equal-face cancellation.

Existence of one progress schedule is enough; confluence and uniqueness are not required.

## 1. Equality-lock setup

Let `G` be the connected loopless cubic reduced graph obtained from one nondegenerate two-vertex cancellation. Let

\[
x:E(G)\to R_5
\]

be a root-valued flow. Let the two marked reconnection edges be

\[
e,f
\]

and assume

\[
x(e)=x(f)=r=uv.
\]

The six equality repair roots are

\[
S_r=\{uw,vw:w\in W\},
\qquad
W=[5]\setminus\{u,v\}.
\]

For `s=ab in S_r`, put

\[
H_s=F_a\mathbin\triangle F_b
   =\{h:x(h)+s\in R_5\}.
\]

Assume the equality expansion survives every one-component rescue and the complete four-port profile lies in the fixed-route outer sector for the original cap matching `M_i`. Then, for every `s in S_r`:

1. `e,f` lie in one connected component
   \[
   Z_s\subseteq H_s;
   \]
2. cutting `e,f` in `Z_s` gives the same oriented route `M_i`;
3. switching `Z_s` gives another root-valued cover in boundary state `A`.

The switched marked values are

\[
(r+s,r+s),
\]

so equality persists.

## 3. Root rigidity applies verbatim

Let

\[
T=\mathcal H\cap M_G
\]

be the pure translation kernel.

Every `v in T` is represented by a physical switch loop with trivial support permutation. Hence

\[

\[
T_a=\{v(a):v\in T\}\le E_5.

\[
T_a=0
\qquad\text{or}\qquad
T_a=E_5.

The second alternative is impossible: the affine coset

\[
x(a)+E_5=E_5

contains zero and co-roots, whereas every translated physical cover is root-valued.

\[
\boxed{T=0.}

\boxed{z(g)=(1+g)y}

for every `g in S_5`.

\rho=x+y.

\[
\boxed{x^g=y+g\rho.}

Both `y` and `rho` are `E_5`-valued flows.

## 4. Edgewise orbit classification

For every edge `a` and every `g in S_5`, the value

### Theorem 4.1 — complete affine-orbit table

For `Y,R in E_5`, the condition

\[
Y+gR\in R_5
\qquad(\forall g\in S_5)
\]

holds exactly in the following two cases:

\[
\boxed{Y=0,\quad R\in R_5,}
\]

or

\[
\boxed{Y\in R_5,\quad R=0.}
\]

There are exactly twenty ordered pairs.

### Proof

The `S_5`-orbits in `E_5` are:

\[
\{0\},
\qquad
R_5,
\qquad
Q_5
\]

where `Q_5` is the five-element co-root orbit.

- If `R=0`, the condition is exactly `Y in R_5`.
- If `R` is a root, its orbit is all ten roots. `Y=0` works. A root `Y` fails on the orbit point `Y`, giving zero. A co-root `Y=q_i` fails on every orbit root containing the missing index `i`, giving a co-root rather than a root.
- If `R` is a co-root, its orbit is all five co-roots. `Y=0` fails. A co-root `Y` fails when the orbit point equals `Y`. A root `Y=ij` fails on a co-root missing `i` or `j`.

10\text{ pairs }(0,R),\ R\in R_5,

## 5. Flow conservation forces a global choice

At one cubic vertex, the three values of the physical root flow `x` are three distinct roots

\[
a,b,c
\]

with

\[
a+b+c=0.
\]

The edgewise classification partitions these three incident edges between the support of `y` and the support of `rho`. Since `y` and `rho` are each flows, the roots assigned to either one must sum to zero.

### Lemma 5.1 — no mixed root-triangle partition

For a root triangle `{a,b,c}`, the only subsets whose sum is zero are

\[
\varnothing
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\{a,b,c\}.
\]

### Proof

A one-element subset is nonzero. The sum of any two roots is the third root and is nonzero. The complete triple sums to zero. ∎

Therefore, at every cubic vertex, either:

- all three incident edges have `y=0` and `rho=x`; or
- all three have `rho=0` and `y=x`.

Adjacent vertices share an edge, so they make the same choice. Since `G` is connected:

### Theorem 5.2 — global dichotomy

Exactly one of

\[
\boxed{y=0,\quad\rho=x}
\]

or

\[
\boxed{y=x,\quad\rho=0}
\]

holds on every edge of `G`.

## 8. Channel conjugation law

The following identity controls how the six cycles change under one common switch.

Let `t` be a root, let `Z` be a connected component of `H_t(x)`, and put

\[
x'=x+t1_Z.
\]

Let `tau_t` be the support transposition associated with `t`.

### Theorem 8.1 — physical channel conjugation

For every root `s`:

\[
\boxed{
H_s(x')
=
\bigl(H_s(x)\setminus Z\bigr)
\cup
\bigl(H_{\tau_t(s)}(x)\cap Z\bigr).
}
\]

### Proof

Outside `Z`, the flow is unchanged. On an edge of `Z`, membership in `H_t` implies that the original root meets `t` in exactly one support index. Hence

\[
x'=x+t=\tau_t x.
\]

Since support permutations preserve `R_5`:

\[
x'+s\in R_5
\iff
\tau_t x+s\in R_5
\iff
x+\tau_t(s)\in R_5.
\]

This is precisely membership in `H_(tau_t(s))(x)` on `Z`. ∎

### Interpretation

One common equality switch does not create a new channel alphabet. It cuts the next channel along `Z` and replaces the inside portion by the conjugate channel. All remaining difficulty is the graph incidence of this splice.

## 9. Common intersection and parity sum

### Theorem 9.1 — common-safe intersection

For the active root `r`:

\[
\boxed{
\bigcap_{s\in S_r}H_s
=x^{-1}(r).}
\]

### Proof

Edgewise, let the root value be `a`.

- If `a=r`, then `a+s` is a root for every `s` meeting `r`.
- If `a` is another root meeting `r`, choose `s=a`; then `a+s=0`.
- If `a` is disjoint from `r`, choose a channel `s in S_r` disjoint from `a`; then `a+s` is a co-root.

Thus `a` is safe for all six channels exactly when `a=r`. ∎

At a cubic root triangle, a fixed root occurs on at most one incident edge. Hence:

### Corollary 9.2

The common intersection of the six locked cycles is a matching. In particular, after leaving a marked `r`-edge, the six cycles have no common continuation edge.

### Theorem 9.3 — six-channel parity identity

\[
\boxed{
\bigtriangleup_{s\in S_r}H_s=H_r.}
\]

### Proof

For one edge value `a`, the number of channels `s in S_r` for which `a+s` is a root is:

\[
6,\quad3,\quad4
\]

according as `a=r`, `a` meets `r`, or `a` is disjoint from `r`. Its parity is therefore one exactly when `a` meets `r` and is distinct from `r`, which is the condition `a in H_r`. ∎

## 10. The endpoint three--three fan

Let a marked `r=uv` edge meet one endpoint vertex whose local root triangle is

\[
\{uv,uw,vw\}
\]

for a unique

\[
w\in W.
\]

The six cycles leave the endpoint through the two non-`r` arms in two groups.

Define

\[
A_w
=
\{vw\}
\cup
\{ux:x\in W\setminus\{w\}\},
\]

and

\[
B_w
=
\{uw\}
\cup
\{vx:x\in W\setminus\{w\}\}.
\]

### Theorem 10.1 — exact local fan

- a channel in `A_w` leaves through the `uw` arm;
- a channel in `B_w` leaves through the `vw` arm;
- `A_w` and `B_w` are complementary three-element transversals of the resolution pairs
  \[
  \{ux,vx\}
  \qquad(x\in W).
  \]

### Proof

For a channel `ux`:

- if `x=w`, the root `uw` is equal to the shift and is unsafe, while `vw` is safe;
- if `x ne w`, `uw+ux=wx` is a root, while `vw+ux` is a co-root.

The `vx` cases are symmetric. ∎

These transversals are the exact Type-X channel profiles:

\[
\boxed{A_w=\delta(uw)=U_r(vw),}
\]

and

\[
\boxed{B_w=\delta(vw)=U_r(uw).}
\]

Indeed

\[
\Psi_r(vw)=vw+r=uw,
\qquad
\Psi_r(uw)=uw+r=vw.
\]

### Corollary 10.2 — endpoint DDD discrepancy

For distinct `w,w' in W`:

\[
\boxed{
A_w\mathbin\triangle A_{w'}
=
B_w\mathbin\triangle B_{w'}
=
\Gamma_{ww'},
}
\]

where

\[
\Gamma_{ww'}
=
\{uw,uw',vw,vw'\}
\]

is the nonzero `K_{2,3}` channel-cohomology class corresponding to the disjoint root `ww'`.

Thus two endpoint fans are either identical or differ by one exact DDD triality direction. No other endpoint mismatch exists.

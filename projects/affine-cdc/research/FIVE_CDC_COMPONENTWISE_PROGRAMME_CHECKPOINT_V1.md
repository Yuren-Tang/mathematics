## Topological bridge

A root lift is equivalent to a cycle-face embedding of the cubic source graph with a proper coloring of its faces by at most eight support indices. Dually it is a closed triangular cellulation with a proper eight-coloring.

For a fixed Fano flow, an admissible gauge word is exactly a code-filtered partial Petrial: its edge bit records whether the two incident face sides are preserved or swapped across that source edge.

Thus:

- support-cycle pivots preserve the uncolored dual triangulation and recolor one dual vertex;
- gauge moves preserve the Fano flow and change the embedding by an admissible partial Petrial;
- connected-cycle switches change the Fano flow and therefore change the available Petrial code in the new fibre.

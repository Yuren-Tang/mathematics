# Five-support covers as cycle-continuous maps to the cographic matroid of `K_5`

**Programme:** `AffineCDC — Research Lead`  
**Status:** theorem-level research checkpoint; not canonical pending literature audit

## 1. Edge-pair labelling

Let `G` be a finite loopless multigraph and let `S` be a five-element set. A
five-support double cover

\[
(F_i)_{i\in S}
\]

assigns to every edge `e∈E(G)` the two-element set of support indices containing
it. Thus it defines an edge map

\[
\phi:E(G)\longrightarrow \binom S2=E(K_S).
\]

Conversely, any edge map to `E(K_S)` defines five edge supports

\[
F_i:=\phi^{-1}(\delta_{K_S}(i)),
\]

where `\delta_{K_S}(i)` is the star cut at `i`.

## 2. The cut-to-cycle theorem

### Theorem 2.1

For a finite loopless multigraph `G`, the following are equivalent.

1. `G` has a double cover by five even edge supports.
2. There is an edge map
   \[
   \phi:E(G)\to E(K_5)
   \]
   such that the inverse image of every cut of `K_5` is an even edge support of
   `G`.
3. There is an edge map `\phi:E(G)\to E(K_5)` such that the inverse image of
   each of the five vertex stars of `K_5` is even.
4. There is a cycle-continuous map of binary matroids
   \[
   M(G)\longrightarrow M^*(K_5),
   \]
   with underlying edge map `\phi`.

#### Proof

`(1)⇒(3)`: label the five cover occurrences by the vertices of `K_5`, and send
each graph edge to the pair of labels of the two supports containing it. The
inverse image of the star at `i` is exactly `F_i`, hence is even.

`(3)⇒(1)`: define `F_i` as the inverse image of the star at `i`. Every graph edge
maps to one edge of `K_5` and therefore belongs to exactly the two supports
corresponding to its endpoints.

The cut space of `K_5` is generated by its vertex stars, so `(2)` and `(3)` are
equivalent. The cycles of the cographic matroid `M^*(K_5)` are precisely the cut
space of `K_5`; therefore `(2)` is exactly the cycle-continuity condition in
`(4)`. ∎

The target is genuinely cographic rather than graphic: `M^*(K_5)` is not the
cycle matroid of a graph because `K_5` is nonplanar.

## 3. Cubic local form

Assume `G` is cubic. At a vertex `v`, the three incident graph edges map to a
three-edge binary cycle of `K_5`, because the inverse image of every cut is even
at `v`. A three-edge cycle of `K_5` is a triangle. Hence:

\[
\boxed{
\text{the three labels incident with every cubic vertex are the three edges of a
triangle of }K_5.
}
\]

Repeated target edges cannot occur in the local triple: after cancellation they
would leave a nonzero one-edge boundary in `K_5`.

This is the direct matroidal form of the anisotropic `O^-(4,2)` flow condition.
The incidence vectors of the ten edges of `K_5` are exactly the ten anisotropic
vectors of the deleted permutation module

\[
H_5=\{z\in\mathbf F_2^5:\sum_i z_i=0\}.
\]

## 4. General `k`-support version

For any finite index set `S`, define

\[
H_S:=\{z\in\mathbf F_2^S:\sum_{s\in S}z_s=0\}
\]

and let the roots be the weight-two vectors `e_s+e_t`. The same argument gives:

### Proposition 4.1

A double cover by `|S|` even supports is equivalent to each of the following.

1. A root-valued `H_S`-flow.
2. An edge map `E(G)\to E(K_S)` pulling every cut of `K_S` back to a cycle of
   `G`.
3. A cycle-continuous binary-matroid map
   \[
   M(G)\to M^*(K_S).
   \]

Thus the number of support occurrences is the size of the complete-graph target
in a cographic cycle-continuity problem.

## 5. Relation to Petersen colouring

Jaeger's Petersen-colouring conjecture asks for a cycle-continuous edge map from
a bridgeless cubic graph to the Petersen graph. The Petersen graph itself has a
five-support double cover, hence by Theorem 2.1 it admits a cycle-continuous
binary-matroid map

\[
M(P)\longrightarrow M^*(K_5).
\]

Composition gives

\[
M(G)\longrightarrow M(P)\longrightarrow M^*(K_5),
\]

and therefore a five-support double cover of `G`. This recovers conceptually the
known implication

\[
\text{Petersen colouring}\Longrightarrow 5\text{-CDC}.
\]

The present formulation does not claim that the intermediate map
`M(P)→M^*(K_5)` is canonical or `S_5`-equivariant; any fixed five-support cover
of the Petersen graph supplies one.

## 6. Strategic meaning

The five-support conjecture is not merely an optimization inside an arbitrary
CDC. It is the assertion that every bridgeless cubic graphic matroid admits a
cycle-continuous map into one fixed ten-element regular matroid:

\[
\boxed{M^*(K_5).}
\]

The universal `O^+(6,2)` AffineCDC root lift instead gives a map to the cographic
matroid of `K_8`, with the additional Fano/Hamming quotient structure. Passing
from eight supports to five asks whether the map can be compressed from
`M^*(K_8)` to an induced `M^*(K_5)` target.

This suggests three further directions:

1. study composition, sums, and cut interfaces in the category of binary
   cycle-continuous matroid maps;
2. determine whether the AffineCDC collapse operation is functorial at this
   cographic target level;
3. compare the exact terminology with tension-continuous, flow-continuous, and
   matroid-homomorphism literature before canonical promotion.

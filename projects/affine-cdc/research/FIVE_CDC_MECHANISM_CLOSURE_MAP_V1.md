# Mechanism and closure map for the AffineCDC five-support programme

**Programme:** `AffineCDC — Research Lead`  
**Status:** research-control synthesis; theorem, exact-computation, and conjectural layers are separated below  
**Purpose:** distinguish genuine structural compression from laboratory compression, and identify which parts of the programme are finite and closed versus globally open

## 1. Why this ledger is needed

The programme uses exact computation aggressively, but its mathematical value depends on converting observed patterns into invariant mechanisms. A shrinking list of examples is not by itself progress toward a general theorem.

This ledger classifies every major layer by two independent questions:

1. **epistemic source:** theorem, exhaustive finite computation, or exploratory evidence;
2. **scope:** universal, closed finite interface, graph-specific complete census, or open global bridge.

The key distinction is:

\[
\boxed{
\text{compression of data}
\ne
\text{compression of the mathematical possibility space}.
}
\]

## 2. Universal theorem backbone

The following mechanisms no longer depend on the laboratories that suggested them.

### 2.1 Root lifts and cycle-face surfaces

A compatible affine root lift is equivalent to a coloured cycle-face embedding. Its dual is a closed triangular cellulation whose vertices are support-cycle components.

The true five-support criterion is

\[
T_g^{(1)}\longrightarrow\mathscr A_5.
\]

This replaces the older global colour-quotient criterion and applies to every compatible root lift.

**Status:** universal theorem.  
**Compression type:** genuine change of language and quantifier.

### 2.2 Gauge moves and partial Petrials

The reduced fixed-flow gauge code is exactly the family of partial edge-twist sets accessible while the Fano flow remains fixed.

**Status:** universal theorem.  
**Compression type:** algebraic and topological mechanisms identified.

### 2.3 Marked-core occurrence loci

For a marked face core `C`, its occurrence set over the gauge torsor is affine, with direction

\[
L_C=B_f\cap\mathbf F_2^{R_C},
\]

where `R_C` is the reserve outside the core face zone.

**Status:** universal theorem.  
**Compression type:** arbitrary persistence questions become shortened-code questions.

### 2.4 Cyclic three-cut reduction

Every weight-six `K_4` reserve direction behind a connected marked core exposes a cyclic three-edge cut. Five-support covers glue across such cuts.

Hence no such line can occur in a vertex-minimal counterexample.

**Status:** universal theorem.  
**Compression type:** an observed obstruction species is eliminated structurally.

### 2.5 Four-edge boundary language

A four-pole has exactly ten labelled support-unordered boundary states, belonging to four unlabelled types `A,B,C,D`. Gluing across a four-edge cut is exactly signature-set intersection.

Cap forcing and support-Kempe path switching impose finite closure rules on those ten states.

**Status:** universal finite-interface theorems.  
**Compression type:** an unbounded graph fragment is represented by a subset of a ten-state alphabet.

### 2.6 Dominating-clique capacity

For `2\le r\le5`,

\[
K_r\vee H\longrightarrow\mathscr A_5
\iff
\chi(H)\le5-r.
\]

Thus every target obstruction possessing a dominating clique of rank at least two is completely classified by one chromatic threshold.

**Status:** universal theorem.  
**Compression type:** infinite obstruction families collapse to one formula.

## 3. Closed finite universes

These are genuinely complete within their stated interfaces.

### 3.1 The four-pole boundary state space

The alphabet

\[
\mathcal S=\{A,B_i,C_i,D_i:i=0,1,2\}
\]

has ten states. All subsets, Kempe-closure rules, cap-forcing conditions, and disjoint profile pairs can be exhausted.

The current abstract irreducible boundary candidates are four Kempe-stable mismatch kernels.

**Closed:** yes, as a state-space classification.  
**Not closed:** which of those profiles are realizable by arbitrary cubic four-poles.

### 3.2 Half-cube clique links

The links of `K_r` for `1\le r\le5` are explicitly known. Ranks two through five are fully decided by chromatic number.

**Closed:** ranks two through five.  
**Remaining boundary:** rank one, namely homomorphism to `J(5,2)=L(K_5)`.

### 3.3 Complete graph-specific censuses

For a fixed finite graph, complete Fano-flow and root-lift enumeration is a closed finite problem.

Examples completed:

- Petersen: all `28560` nowhere-zero Fano flows;

These are complete theorems about those finite objects, not universal graph theorems.

## 4. Laboratory compression versus structural compression

### 4.1 Structural compression

A compression is structural when it supplies an invariant theorem that applies before any instance is enumerated.

Examples:

- `K_4` reserve line `\Rightarrow` cyclic three-cut;
- four-pole signatures form a ten-state interface;
- `K_r\vee H` is decided by `\chi(H)` for `r\ge2`;
- marked-core persistence equals a shortened gauge code.

These results reduce the mathematical possibility space.

### 4.2 Laboratory compression

A compression is laboratory-specific when it classifies every case in one finite graph or neighbourhood but does not yet constrain arbitrary graphs.

Examples:

- the universal three-cube and six-cube weighted-dual modules;
- the fact that the thirty-vertex residual neighbourhood used only points and `K_4` lines;
- the fact that every local `K_6`-free bad lift around the chosen `J_5` witness is the same `D_9`;
- the eight small-four-pole profiles through six internal vertices.

These results are mathematically exact, but their generality remains a theorem target rather than an established fact.

### 4.3 Exploratory evidence

Sampling larger four-poles, larger snarks, or random Fano flows is used to discover the next invariant and to falsify overgeneralizations.

It is not promoted to a general conclusion unless converted into a proof or an explicitly exhaustive finite census.

## 5. Are we in a finite closed range?

The answer has three levels.

### 5.1 Locally finite and closed

The following are finite:

- the target graph `\mathscr A_5`;
- every clique-link table;
- every fixed graph's Fano-flow space;
- every fixed flow's gauge torsor;
- the four-pole ten-state boundary alphabet;
- the abstract Kempe/cap closure problem on that alphabet.

### 5.2 Globally unbounded but finitely interfaced

Compatible dual triangulations can have arbitrarily many vertices and faces. Fano-flow reconfiguration graphs and four-pole compositions are likewise unbounded.

The programme attempts to control this unboundedness using finite interfaces:

\[
\text{large graph}
\longmapsto
\text{flow/gauge module}
\longmapsto
\text{marked core type}
\longmapsto
\text{boundary signature or target link capacity}.
\]

This is analogous to a finite-state description of an infinite family; the global composition theorem is not yet complete.

### 5.3 Not a finite forbidden-subgraph list

There are already infinite obstruction families such as

\[
K_3\vee C_{2k+1}.
\]

However these are not evidence of uncontrolled list growth, because all are compressed by the exact rank-three bipartiteness criterion.

A genuinely new family must escape the existing mechanism language, for example by having:

- no dominating clique of size at least two;
- a rank-one remainder failing to map to `L(K_5)`;
- or global renewal whose marked-core cover cannot be decomposed into the current finite interfaces.

## 6. Current dependence on exploration and mechanism

It is misleading to assign one global percentage. The dependence is layered.

### Discovery frontier

Exploration is currently essential for:

- locating the first compatible obstruction outside the `K_6` family;
- finding candidate universal modules;
- testing full-cap-profile hypotheses;
- locating bad-flow components in larger snarks.

### Durable backbone

The central reductions are now mechanism-driven:

- topology and gauge correspondence;
- affine occurrence loci;
- cut gluing;
- finite four-pole signatures;
- Kempe closure;
- exact dominating-clique capacities.

### Present bottleneck

The missing statements are not more census totals. They are bridge theorems showing that the finite local mechanisms cover every global obstruction:

1. full-cap-profile theorem for arbitrary admissible four-poles;
2. classification or exclusion of compatible bad duals without a dominating clique of rank at least two;
3. horizontal escape-or-decomposition for graph-level bad-flow sets.

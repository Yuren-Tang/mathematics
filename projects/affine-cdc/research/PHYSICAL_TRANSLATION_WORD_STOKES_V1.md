## 1. Standard affine `A3` lift

Use the affine Coxeter generators

\[
s_1=(12),\qquad s_2=(23),\qquad s_3=(34),
\]

and the affine reflection `s0` whose linear part is `(14)` and whose translation part is

\[
\theta=e_1-e_4.
\]

Thus

\[
W(\widetilde A_3)=Q(A_3)\rtimes S_4,
\]

with multiplication

\[
(\mu,\sigma)(\nu,\tau)
=(\mu+\sigma\nu,\sigma\tau).
\]

Write

\[
\nu(s_i)=0\quad(i=1,2,3),
\qquad
\nu(s_0)=\theta.
\]

Let

\[
w=s_{i_1}s_{i_2}\cdots s_{i_n}
\]

and let

\[
\sigma_k
\]

be the finite `S4` image of the prefix `s_(i1)...s_(ik)`, with `sigma_0=1`.

### Theorem 1.1 — explicit translation cocycle

The affine translation part of `w` is

\[
\boxed{
\mu(w)
=
\sum_{k=1}^n
\sigma_{k-1}\nu(s_{i_k})
=
\sum_{k:i_k=0}
\sigma_{k-1}\theta.
}
\]

### Proof

This is the semidirect-product recursion

\[
(\mu_{k-1},\sigma_{k-1})(\nu(s_{i_k}),s_{i_k})
=
(\mu_{k-1}+\sigma_{k-1}\nu(s_{i_k}),
 \sigma_{k-1}s_{i_k}).
\]

Iterating gives the formula.

### Cocycle law

For two words `u,v`,

\[
\boxed{
\mu(uv)=\mu(u)+\rho(u)\mu(v),
}
\]

where `rho(u)` is the finite `S4` transport of `u`.

In particular, on the identity-transport kernel,

\[
\rho(u)=\rho(v)=1,
\]

one has ordinary additivity

\[
\boxed{
\mu(uv)=\mu(u)+\mu(v).
}
\]

## 2. The finite translation word

Reduction modulo two gives

\[
\bar\mu(w)
\in
Q(A_3)/2Q(A_3)
\cong
E_4
=
\left\{
(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)\in\mathbf F_2^4:
\sum_i x_i=0
\right\}.
\]

Let the four locked challenges be the four points of

\[
\Lambda\cong AG(2,2).
\]

Use the established `S4`-equivariant identification

\[
E_4
\cong
\operatorname{Aff}(\Lambda,\mathbf F_2).
\]

### Definition 2.1 — frame translation word

For an identity-transport overlap frame word `w`, define

\[
\boxed{
\Theta_{\mathrm{fr}}(w)
\in
\operatorname{Aff}(\Lambda,\mathbf F_2)
}
\]

as the affine four-bit word corresponding to `bar mu(w)`.

### Theorem 2.2

`Theta_fr` is a homomorphism from the identity-transport word group to the affine four-bit code:

\[
\boxed{
\Theta_{\mathrm{fr}}(uv)
=
\Theta_{\mathrm{fr}}(u)
+
\Theta_{\mathrm{fr}}(v).
}
\]

It is equivariant under change of support frame by `S4`.

## 3. Generator values

The three translation hexagons from the affine-kernel dossier satisfy

\[
u_1=t_{e_2-e_1},
\qquad
u_2=t_{e_3-e_2},
\qquad
u_3=t_{e_4-e_3}.
\]

Hence

\[
\boxed{
\bar\mu(u_1)=1100,
\qquad
\bar\mu(u_2)=0110,
\qquad
\bar\mu(u_3)=0011,
}
\]

up to the fixed `S4` identification of the four support coordinates with the four challenge points.

These three weight-two affine words are independent and generate the full even/affine code.

### Consequence 3.1 — bounded generator reduction

Any additive, `S4`-equivariant candidate physical challenge-word map on identity-return overlap words which

1. vanishes on backtracks;
2. is invariant under commuting-square order exchange;
3. is invariant under the adjacent braid order exchange;
4. agrees with `Theta_fr` on `u1,u2,u3`;

must agree with `Theta_fr` on every identity-transport word.

### Proof

The identity kernel is generated by conjugates of the Coxeter relators together with `u1,u2,u3`. The first three assumptions kill the Coxeter-relator part; the fourth fixes a basis of the translation lattice modulo two.

### Significance

The infinite physical translation-word theorem is reduced to three bounded six-step source configurations, plus the already isolated square and braid order surgeries.

## 4. Finite regional Stokes theorem

Form a two-complex whose one-skeleton is the full frame Cayley graph and whose bounded cells are:

- backtrack cells;
- commuting-square cells;
- braid/doubled-face cells;
- translated conjugates of the three translation hexagons.

Label the first three cell types by zero and label a conjugate `g ui g^{-1}` by

\[
 g\bar\mu(u_i)
\in E_4.
\]

### Theorem 4.1 — abstract mod-two Stokes

For every finite two-chain `D` in this complex,

\[
\boxed{
\Theta_{\mathrm{fr}}(\partial D)
=
\sum_{F\subset D}
\operatorname{label}(F).
}
\]

### Proof

Internal oriented edges cancel in the boundary. Translation words add on identity loops, and the cell labels are their translation words. The zero-labelled Coxeter cells contribute nothing.

### Trust boundary

This is a theorem in the finite frame/affine-Coxeter complex. A source-graph regional Stokes theorem still requires a functorial assignment of actual side attachments to these cell labels.

## 5. Curvature does not admit a canonical four-bit origin

Let

\[
C=\mathbf F_2^\Lambda,
\qquad
A=\operatorname{Aff}(\Lambda,\mathbf F_2)
=
\ker\left(\sum:C\to\mathbf F_2\right).
\]

There is an exact sequence of `S4`-modules

\[
\boxed{
0\longrightarrow A
\longrightarrow C
\xrightarrow{\operatorname{curv}}
\mathbf F_2
\longrightarrow0.
}
\]

### Theorem 5.1 — nonsplitting

This exact sequence has no `S4`-equivariant linear splitting.

### Proof

An equivariant section would send `1 in F2` to an `S4`-fixed vector of odd weight in `C`. The only `S4`-fixed vectors are

\[
0000
\quad\text{and}\quad
1111,
\]

and both have even total parity. Contradiction.

### Consequence 5.2 — the correct Stokes target is a torsor

The odd-curvature fibre

\[
C_1
=
\{f\in C:\operatorname{curv}(f)=1\}
\]

has no canonical `S4`-equivariant origin. It is naturally an affine torsor over

\[
A
\cong
Q(A_3)/2Q(A_3).
\]

Therefore:

- an identity-holonomy remainder has a canonical flat translation word in `A`;
- an odd DDD return determines only a point of the odd torsor `C1`;
- choosing a particular three-step DDD curvature core chooses a temporary origin of `C1`;
- replacing that core by another changes the representative by one flat translation word.

This explains why the decomposition

\[
\text{odd return}
=
\text{bounded DDD core}
+
\text{identity remainder}
\]

is exact but not canonically split.

## 6. Revised physical theorem

The earlier formulation asked for an absolute map

\[
\Theta_{\mathrm{phys}}:
Q(A_3)\to\mathbf F_2^\Lambda
\]

and a canonical odd extension. The finite algebra shows that the second demand is impossible without extra physical side data.

The correct source-graph theorem has two parts.

### Target 6.1 — flat translation comparison

For every physical identity-return overlap region, construct its actual four-challenge side-choice word

\[
B_{\mathrm{phys}}(\gamma)
\in A
\]

and prove

\[
\boxed{
B_{\mathrm{phys}}(\gamma)
=
\Theta_{\mathrm{fr}}(\gamma).
}
\]

By Consequence 3.1 it is enough to prove this on:

- the three translation hexagons `u1,u2,u3`;
- the bounded square and braid order moves.

### Target 6.2 — torsor-valued regional Stokes

For a physical region with possibly odd DDD curvature, assign a side-choice element

\[
B_{\mathrm{phys}}(R)
\in
C_{\kappa(R)},
\]

where

\[
C_0=A,
\qquad
C_1\text{ is the odd torsor},
\]

such that gluing regions adds their affine differences and

\[
\operatorname{curv}(B_{\mathrm{phys}}(R))
=
\sum_{F\subset R}\kappa(F).
\]

No equivariant absolute odd representative should be required.

### Target 6.3 — physical consequences

Once Target 6.1 is proved:

- `0000` is the removable/zero translation class;
- `1111` is the global-complement/common-cut class;
- every weight-two word selects one canonical terminal matching and feeds the Type-T serial/four-pole theorem;
- odd torsor classes feed the bounded DDD blossom branch.

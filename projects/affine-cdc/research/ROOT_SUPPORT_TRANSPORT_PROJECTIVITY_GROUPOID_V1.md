## 1. Facet perspectivity in the root triangle complex

Let two adjacent source vertices carry distinct support triangles

\[
\Delta=ijk,
\qquad
\Delta'=ij\ell,
\qquad k\ne\ell,
\]

and share the root edge `ij`.

In the triangle pseudocomplex, the facets `Delta,Delta'` meet along the ridge `ij`.

The standard facet perspectivity

\[
\langle\Delta,\Delta'\rangle:
V(\Delta)\longrightarrow V(\Delta')
\]

fixes the shared vertices `i,j` and sends the unique opposite vertex

\[
k\longmapsto\ell.
\]

Because all vertices already carry support labels in `[5]`, this partial bijection has one canonical extension to a permutation of the full support set:

\[
\boxed{
\widehat{\langle\Delta,\Delta'\rangle}
=(k\ \ell)\in S_5.}
\]

It fixes all support labels other than `k,ell`.

### Theorem 1.1 — local projectivity/support-transport identity

The support transposition attached to crossing one glued root side between two distinct root triangles is exactly the canonical `S_5` extension of the facet perspectivity across that side.

Equal adjacent triangles contribute the identity projectivity before their two-face cancellation.

## 2. Projectivity along a source dual path

Let

\[
\gamma=(\Delta_0,\Delta_1,\ldots,\Delta_m)
\]

be a path of adjacent support triangles in the root pseudocomplex. Define

\[
\operatorname{Proj}(\gamma)
=
\widehat{\langle\Delta_{m-1},\Delta_m\rangle}
\cdots
\widehat{\langle\Delta_0,\Delta_1\rangle}
\in S_5.
\]

This is the `S_5`-extended facet projectivity of `gamma`.

### Theorem 2.1 — global identity

The support permutation transported along a root-triangle history path is

\[
\boxed{
\Pi(\gamma)=\operatorname{Proj}(\gamma).}
\]

### Proof

Both quantities are defined by composing, in path order, the same local transposition exchanging the two opposite support labels and fixing the common root side. ∎

Thus the support holonomy used in the Ptolemy/Petersen dossiers is not an independent affine decoration. It is the ordinary projectivity holonomy of the five-colored triangle pseudocomplex, expressed in the global support-label set.

## 3. Relation to Joswig's projectivity group

For a strongly connected pure simplicial complex, Joswig's projectivity group is generated by compositions of perspectivities along closed facet paths.

For a connected component of the split root triangle pseudocomplex and a base triangle `Delta_0`, the present extended support holonomies form the image

\[
\widehat\Pi(\mathfrak T_\lambda,\Delta_0)
\le S_5
\]

of the standard projectivity group under canonical extension from the three vertices of each traversed facet to the five support labels.

The extension records more ambient color labels than the abstract projectivity permutation on one base triangle, but no new local transport choice is introduced.

### Scope note

The root pseudocomplex may have singular vertex links and boundary. Apply the projectivity construction componentwise after splitting singular vertices, or formulate it directly on the strongly connected facet groupoid. Boundary facets and the ordered cap context are retained as relative data.

## 4. Co-root token transport as a projectivity action

A co-root atom has two crossed root resolutions

\[
F=\{s,t\},
\qquad s\perp t,
\]

which form one Petersen edge.

At one nontrivial critical overlap, consecutive atom states are

\[
F=\{s,t\}
\longrightarrow
F'=\{t,u\}.
\]

The forced-backbone transport theorem assigns one support transposition

\[
\tau_z\in S_5
\]

with

\[
\tau_z(t)=t,
\qquad
\tau_z(s)=u.
\]

The complete labelled critical neighbourhood consists of adjacent support triangles whose facet perspectivities compose to the same transposition `tau_z`.

### Theorem 4.1 — token transport is induced projectivity

On every verified forced-backbone critical cell, the bijection

\[
\{X_F,Y_F\}\longrightarrow\{X_{F'},Y_{F'}\}
\]

of crossed root-resolution routes is the action induced by the local facet projectivity product on the resolution fibre.

Consequently a forced co-root track is a path in a two-sheeted local system associated to the relative facet-projectivity groupoid.

### Trust boundary

The theorem identifies the transport once the forced-backbone cell has been established. It does not prove that every trapped critical overlap is forced-backbone; that remains the source-level six-port classification.

## 5. Cap orientation as relative projectivity reduction

The original two-vertex cap distinguishes:

1. one terminal matching;
2. one block of that matching.

Hence physical transport in an oriented lock is restricted to the subgroup of the projectivity image stabilizing the distinguished block.

Let

\[
\nu:\widehat\Pi\longrightarrow\mathbf F_2
\]

be the block-exchange character on the selected crossed route.

The oriented-channel calibration theorem gives

\[
\boxed{
\nu(\operatorname{Proj}(\gamma))=0
}
\]

for every pivot-closed physical subtrack `gamma`.

The Petersen resolution-parity theorem identifies

\[
\nu(\operatorname{Proj}(\gamma))
=|\gamma|\pmod2.
\]

Therefore every pivot-closed physical subtrack has even length.

### Interpretation

Hypothesis `O+` of the confluence theorem is exactly a relative projectivity-triviality statement on one two-point quotient of the facet-projectivity groupoid.

It does not require the entire `S_5` projectivity to be trivial; only the cap-selected block character must vanish.

## 7. What projectivity cannot remove

Projectivity is holonomy, not a colored-weld theorem.

Even when the relevant projectivity character is trivial, one still needs to convert an even recurrent carrier into strict progress on the source graph.

The controlling proof uses:

\[
C_6
\longrightarrow
\text{relative root NNI}
\longrightarrow
\text{canonical star}
\longrightarrow
\text{equal-face cancellation},
\]

and the seam-compatible reduction

\[
C_8\longrightarrow C_6+C_6.
\]

These are source movies retaining all side attachments. They are not consequences of projectivity-group triviality.

### Proposition 7.1 — holonomy/weld separation

Vanishing relative projectivity removes odd return and orientation obstruction. It does not imply the existence of a root-admissible weld or strict target-order descent.

Thus `O+` and `E` remain logically distinct hypotheses of the confluence theorem.

## 8. Potential further compression

The current simple-cycle language may be replaced by a small relative projectivity 2-complex:

1. vertices: co-root resolution states;
2. edges: critical projectivity steps;
3. orientation character: cap-block action;
4. primitive admissible 2-cells: full-state `C6` cancellation movies;
5. `C8` cells: composites of two primitive `C6` cells.

If constructed source-faithfully, the no-sink theorem would read:

> every projectivity-closed token path is orientation-even and null-homotopic through strict colored-weld cells.

This would be an expositional reformulation of the present Petersen proof. It does not yet shorten the finite source verification.

## 9. PDL reconstruction target

PDL may package the transport layer as follows.

1. Define the facet projectivity across `ijk|ijell` and prove its extension is `(k ell)`.
2. Prove that the existing support transport is the path product of these projectivities.
3. For every critical co-root cell, identify the forced transposition `tau_z` with the local projectivity product.
4. Define the induced two-sheeted resolution local system.
5. Prove the cap block reduces the physical groupoid to the kernel of its orientation character.
6. Derive odd pivot-closed exclusion.
7. Treat even cores by the independent source-movie theorem.

This order removes duplicated transport terminology while preserving the source-level burden.

## 4. Legitimate finite/kernel residuals

A residual may remain delegated when the corpus records:

1. the exact proposition;
2. every hypothesis;
3. its mathematical mechanism;
4. the released declaration;
5. the downstream consumer;
6. the fact that no new external theorem is hidden.

Accepted residual families include:

- rational rearrangements in nondominant forcing;
- integer rounding and threshold closure;
- label/Peierls charge arithmetic;
- exponential-over-polynomial eventual domination;
- inverse-square component sums;
- aggregate Taylor constants;
- final threshold/cardinality maxima;
- finite tuple casts and index identities.

## 7. Manuscript reliability rule

A manuscript may cite the immutable formalization for finite residuals only if it also prints the mathematical statement, hypotheses, and role of the residual.

Formal verification cannot replace a missing paper implication. Conversely, a manuscript's PNT exposition does not change the release axiom audit.
